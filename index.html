🧭 Inquiry-based Learning

Every lesson starts with a question. Students aren't told what to think. They are guided to discover answers through observation, discussion, and exploration.

🤝 Collaborative Thinking

Students work in small groups, sharing perspectives and challenging each other's ideas. They learn that knowledge isn't just about getting the right answer; it's about understanding why it's right.

🔬 Hands-on Research

From simple experiments in primary school to structured research projects in middle school, students learn to gather data, form hypotheses, and draw conclusions from evidence. These are skills that last a lifetime.

🎯 Independent Mindset

The ultimate goal: students who can think for themselves. They learn how to find information, evaluate it critically, and use it with clarity. Over time, this builds confidence and a steady, problem-solving approach to life.

From The Principal

A Message to Parents

At RV Public School, we believe every child is born curious. Our role as educators is not to fill their minds with information, but to kindle the flame of inquiry that already exists within them.

We have built RVPS around a simple principle: if children learn how to think, they can learn anything. Our curriculum is designed to encourage questioning, foster collaboration, and develop the independent thinking skills that will serve them throughout their lives.

From robotics and financial literacy to art-integrated learning and research-based projects, every element of our school is purposefully designed to prepare children not just for exams, but for the world beyond them.

I invite you to visit our campus, meet our teachers, and see for yourself how learning comes alive at RVPS.

Principal, RV Public School
